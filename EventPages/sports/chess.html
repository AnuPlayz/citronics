Description

Rules

1. This is a two player game.
2. The game is played on a checkered board with 64 squares, alternating light and dark.
3. Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns.
4. The goal of the game is to checkmate your opponent's king, which means trapping their king so that it cannot escape capture.
5. The game begins with white making the first move. Players take turns moving their pieces.
6. If a player's king is attacked by an opponent's piece, the king is in check. The player must move their king out of check, block the attack, or capture the attacking piece.
7. If a player cannot move without their king being in check, the game is over and the player is checkmated.
8. Other ways to end the game include resignation, stalemate (when a player cannot make any legal move, but their king is not in check), and a draw by agreement, threefold repetition, or the fifty-move rule.
